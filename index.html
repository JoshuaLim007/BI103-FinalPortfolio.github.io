GENERAL BIOLOGY CONCEPTS

Here I will be sharing answers to two different quiz questios on the life stages of a cell and muscular system. These answers will demonstrate my ability to recall general biology concepts.

Question 1:

Tell a cell's life story, including four different processes (life stages) that occur to that cell over time. You can be creative as long as you include the stages discussed in Guide A, section 4.

A cell undergoes mitosis, where one cell divides and becomes two daughter cells. Then the cell grows in size over time, hypertrophy. Then each cell goes through differentiation, where a cell changes its structure and function over time. After some time the cells dies which is known as apoptosis.

Question 2:

Muscles have a unique structure that is often referred to as “bundles within bundles.” Describe what the bundles are to someone who cannot visualize them; starting from the largest (fascicle) to the smallest (myofilament); including appearance and function.

Bundles within bundles mean that the muscles are made up of many fascicles that are made up of many myocytes that are made up of many myofibrils that are made up of many myofilaments. Each structure are made up of something smaller. That is why it's called bundles within bundles.

Fascicles are stringy connective tissue. Myocytes are also stringy along with myofibrils and filaments. Myofilaments move very little, but with many of these, it can add up to a large contraction.

HUMAN BIOLOGY CONCEPTS

I will be explaining various non-infectious diseases in humans. These explanation will demonstrate my knowledge on diseaes related to the human biology.

Types of Diabetes:

Type 1: Insulin dependent, juvenile onset, beta cells are not producing enough insulin, often related to genetics.

Type 2: Not insulin dependent, adult onset, pancreas produces enough insulin but cells don't have enough receptors to receive it. Age, overweight, lack of exercise, and history themselves can increase risks.

Gestational: People are not making enough insulin or utilize them during pregnancy. Ages over 25 and high weight can increase risks.

Cancers:

For cancer to appear, a cell must undergo a series of mutations to the DNA. This occurs during mitosis or exposure to mutagens such as UV radiation. These mutations must occur in the proto oncogenes region of the DNA, causing the cell not to undergo apoptosis. The cell then will undergo rapid growth creating a tumor. If the cancer cells masterized, then we will call that a stage 4 cancer. Otherwise it will be a stage 2 and 3 for larger tumors and stage 1 for small tumors.
